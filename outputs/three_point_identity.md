His raw PPS (1.046) vs the other perimeter players' volume-weighted 1.101: the gap is real but modest, because his two-point finishing is genuinely good. **Frame it precisely: the issue is shot-mix value against the system's target mix, not his finishing.** A good two is still worth less than a league-average three, and Brown takes the most two-heavy, long-two-heaviest diet among Boston's wings while using the most shots.

## 3. PROJECTION — how much better without him (labeled, ranged)

_Everything below is a projection. Method: take Brown's 2025-26 volume (21.7 FGA/g at a realized 1.046 PPS) and re-price the same volume under a different mix and accuracy._

- **(a) Paul George's actual 2025-26 mix and accuracy** (3PT rate 0.496, 3P% 0.391, 2P% 0.486 → 1.072 expected PPS): **-0.1 to +0.6 pts/game** (~-0.2 to +1.5 wins).
- **(b) Non-Brown Boston mix and accuracy** (3PT rate 0.520, 3P% 0.370, 2P% 0.564 → 1.120 expected PPS): **+0.8 to +1.6 pts/game** (~+2.2 to +4.3 wins).

Conversions and discounts, documented: pts/game delta × 2.7 wins per point of per-game differential (the same Pythagorean rule of thumb as script 16; defense assumed unchanged). The low corner applies a 50% creation-transfer discount; the George corners also take up to 2pp off his 3P%.

**Caveats that print with the number, not under it:**

- **George is 36 in 2026.** His accuracy is the whole engine of scenario (a); the haircut corner is not pessimism, it's the base case for a 36-year-old's jumper. If his 3P% slides more than 2pp, the low end of (a) is optimistic.
- **Someone must create the extra catch-and-shoot threes.** Brown self-created much of his volume; a catch-and-shoot diet requires a creator to bend the defense first. Creation does not transfer 1:1 — that is what the 50% transfer corner prices, and with Brown gone the creation burden concentrates on Tatum (see tatum_first_option.md §4 on load).
- **Defensive attention shifts.** Brown's gravity and downhill pressure created some of the very catch-and-shoot looks this model re-prices for others; removing him changes the shot quality of the remaining mix in ways a mix-swap cannot see.
- **Free throws excluded.** Brown's rim pressure generated FTs that a three-heavier diet gives back some of; the PPS frame does not credit them on either side.

**Honest bottom line:** through the realistic channel — George's actual mix and accuracy — the swap is **roughly a wash** (-0.1 to +0.6 pts/game once aging and creation discounts bite); only the idealized case where Brown's volume re-prices at the full non-Brown team mix reaches +0.8 to +1.6 pts/game (~+2.2 to +4.3 wins). The diet-swap gain is small; say so plainly. This is a supporting argument for the trade, not the headline one; the configuration evidence in tatum_first_option.md carries more weight.

- Team table: LeagueDashTeamStats Base + Advanced, one call per season per measure, cached. Ranks are league ranks (1 = highest).

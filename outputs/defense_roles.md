# What Boston Lost Defensively — Brown's Defense by Role

_Extends [defense_check.md](defense_check.md), which already refuted the 'declining defense' trade-case angle and concluded defense is closer to a point in Brown's favor. This memo asks the post-trade question instead: what kind of defender left? On-ball vs off-ball, Tatum through the identical code path as benchmark. **Tatum's 2025-26 columns sit on 16 games (injury) — treat them as context, not evidence.**_

## 1. On-ball — the matchup lens

Season matchup totals as the PRIMARY defender (partial possessions), with two difficulty cuts: matchups against high-usage (>25% USG) opponents, and against each opponent team's #1-usage option.

| Metric | Brown 24-25 | Tatum 24-25 | Brown 25-26 | Tatum 25-26 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Partial poss defended | 4315 | 5203 | 4836 | 1040 |
| PPP allowed (all) | 0.21 | 0.16 | 0.19 | 0.14 |
| Matchup FG% (all) | 0.469 | 0.476 | 0.422 | 0.400 |
| TOV forced / 100 poss | 1.9 | 1.9 | 1.7 | 1.5 |
| Share of poss vs USG>25% | 27% | 15% | 18% | 17% |
| PPP allowed vs USG>25% | 0.26 | 0.26 | 0.26 | 0.11 |
| Share of poss vs #1 options | 19% | 8% | 11% | 6% |
| PPP allowed vs #1 options | 0.27 | 0.29 | 0.24 | 0.15 |
| Defended FG% vs norm (all, pts) | +0.0 | +0.3 | -4.2 | -7.3 |
| Defended FG% vs norm (rim, pts) | +0.8 | -0.6 | -5.7 | -3.8 |

**Read (2024-25, both healthy):** Brown spent a clearly larger share of his defended possessions on high-usage players (27% vs 15%) and on opponents' #1 options (19% vs 8%) — **Brown took the harder assignment**. His overall points-allowed rate ran higher than Tatum's (0.21 vs 0.16 per partial poss) — but that comparison is confounded by exactly that assignment difficulty: against the #1 options themselves Brown allowed 0.27 to Tatum's 0.29. In 2025-26, at 35% offensive usage, Brown's defended-FG% margin went from neutral (+0.0) to -4.2 overall and -5.7 at the rim — the improvement defense_check.md already flagged.

## 3. Niche cuts (the two the data supports)

**Screen navigation (Synergy, defending P&R ball-handlers):**

| | Poss | PPP allowed | League pctile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brown 24-25 | 316 | 0.90 | 48% |
| Tatum 24-25 | 271 | 0.92 | 36% |
| Brown 25-26 | 236 | 0.85 | 63% |
| Tatum 25-26 | 53 | 0.93 | 33% |

Brown defended more P&R ball-handler possessions than Tatum in 2024-25 (316 vs 271) at a better PPP (0.90 vs 0.92) — guards hunting the Boston stars got less out of hunting Brown.

**Transition defense (opponent fast-break points per 48, on vs off):**

| | On floor | Off floor | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brown 24-25 | 14.7 | 12.1 | +2.6 |
| Tatum 24-25 | 14.2 | 12.2 | +2.1 |
| Brown 25-26 | 14.0 | 13.4 | +0.5 |
| Tatum 25-26 | 13.1 | 13.8 | -0.8 |

Opponents got more fast-break points with Brown on the floor in both seasons (+2.6 and +0.5 per 48). Read alongside [live_ball_turnovers.md](live_ball_turnovers.md): part of that is his own live-ball giveaways starting the break, not (or not only) a failure to get back — the two files measure the same coin from two sides. Tatum shows the same-signed but smaller effect in 2024-25, and per-48 opponent FB points are pace- and lineup-confounded; deltas this size are suggestive, not damning.

**Cut:** cross-matching / how often each defended up a position — stats.nba.com does not expose matchup position data at reasonable cost; reported as not measured rather than guessed.

## 4. Verdict — what did Boston actually lose?

- **The primary on-ball defender, and a good one.** A 19%-to-8% edge over Tatum in share of possessions spent on opponents' #1 options, slightly better points-allowed against those toughest matchups, better P&R ball-handler defense, and 2025-26 defended-FG% margins (-4.2 overall, -5.7 at the rim) that are legitimately excellent. This is the strongest pro-Brown fact in the project and it is printed prominently on purpose.
- **An ordinary off-ball defender.** Activity and rebounding metrics are fine-not-special; the versatility claim we could not measure is acknowledged above.
- **The honest weighing:** the trade case (shot diet, price, flow) pays for itself or it doesn't — it cannot lean on defense, and post-trade Boston must now cover #1-option assignments that Brown was absorbing. Tatum's matchup profile shows he was NOT doing that job while Brown was here; someone else now has to.

## 5. Methods

- Matchups: LeagueSeasonMatchups season totals (partial possessions; PPP = points allowed / partial poss). High-usage cut at USG>25% from LeagueDashPlayerStats Advanced; '#1 option' = each team's top-USG player with ≥24 MPG and ≥25 GP.
- Defended FG%: PlayerDashPtShotDefend PCT_PLUSMINUS (shooters' FG% vs their season norm when this player is the closest defender).
- Hustle: LeagueHustleStats totals scaled to per-36. Transition: TeamPlayerOnOffDetails 'Misc' OPP_PTS_FB scaled per 48 minutes (not possession-adjusted — noted).
- All pulls cached (`data/cache/`); driver `scripts/18_defense_roles.py`.
